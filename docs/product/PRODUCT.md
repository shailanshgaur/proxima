# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Residents in hyperlocal societies (apartment complexes, gated communities) aged 25-65 who need urgent, nearby services. Context: Tuesday 3pm, AC breaks, need electrician within 2 hours. They value proximity over choice, trust over price, and proof over claims.

## Product Purpose

Proxima is a hyperlocal service marketplace where residents book trusted neighbors (vendors in their society) for urgent home services. Success: residents find vendors 2x faster than calling random plumbers, vendors get repeat bookings from same neighborhood.

## Brand Personality

Warm, nearby, proven. Not sleek SaaS, not a generic marketplace. Feels like asking neighbors for a recommendation, not scrolling Uber.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS (Figma blue, teal primary, white surfaces, card grids)
- Overly formal (banking, clinical, distant)
- Overly playful (doodles, gradients, emoji as icons)
- Invisible unless you search (no community proof, no personalities)

## Design Principles

1. **Proximity first** — every vendor card shows distance; nearest vendor wins by default
2. **Trust through neighbors** — highlight jobs done in YOUR society, specific reviews, vendor names + faces
3. **Speed over perfect** — frictionless booking (3 taps max), confirmation in 15 minutes
4. **Hyperlocal narrative** — design language and copy reflect "find someone nearby, book today" not "find anyone, eventually"
5. **Earned rating** — vendor rating only meaningful if tied to specific work in resident's own neighborhood

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Touch targets ≥48dp. Color not sole conveyor of meaning. Contrast ≥4.5:1 for body text.
